Always / Everything — Twenty Chapters for Laura

Governing line: “Always” is time transfigured into vow; “Everything” is the world transfigured into intimacy. This live draft preserves your 20-chapter arc with concise quotable pulses and explicit placeholders for the pictures you will supply next.

Chapter 1

Before language

Theme: the whole universe hidden in the smallest thing.

“a World in a Grain of Sand” — William Blake, Auguries of Innocence

Chapter 2

The present is eternity’s doorway

Theme: forever is not later; it is now, intensified.

“Forever – is composed of Nows –” — Emily Dickinson

Chapter 3

The self opens into the other

Theme: intimacy as ontological merger, not mere affection.

“belongs to you” — Walt Whitman, Song of Myself

Chapter 4

Nothing is foreign

Theme: everything belongs to one woven whole.

“All things are woven together” — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations

Chapter 5

Always begins as return

Theme: repetition, ritual, recurrence.

“eternity in an hour” — William Blake

Chapter 6

Love enlarges scale

Theme: one person makes the world too large and too intimate at once.

“your heart” — e.e. cummings

Chapter 7

Infinity becomes frightening

Theme: the cosmos as terror before it becomes shelter.

“infinite spaces frightens me” — Blaise Pascal, Pensées

Chapter 8

Love answers fear

Theme: intimacy as the answer to cosmic exposure.

“strong as death” — Song of Songs 8:6

Chapter 9

The vow survives the flood

Theme: “always” tested by force, distance, catastrophe.

“Many waters cannot quench love” — Song of Songs 8:7

Chapter 10

Love outlasts all other powers

Theme: not sentiment, but endurance.

“Love never ends” — 1 Corinthians 13:8

Chapter 11

The ordinary becomes luminous

Theme: flowers, windows, rooms, gestures become total.

“a Heaven in a Wild Flower” — William Blake

Chapter 12

Loss enters the architecture

Theme: every “always” is shadowed by mortality.

“’Tis better to have loved” — Alfred Tennyson, In Memoriam

Chapter 13

Absence becomes a form of fidelity

Theme: separation clarifies rather than annuls the vow.

“the days that are no more” — Alfred Tennyson

Chapter 14

Trust after anguish

Theme: the turn from intensity to faith.

“All shall be well” — Julian of Norwich

Chapter 15

The universe is moved by love

Theme: love as engine of the world.

“moves the sun and the other stars” — Dante, Paradiso

Chapter 16

The beloved becomes a world

Theme: Laura as horizon, weather, and structure.

“Infinity in the palm” — William Blake

Chapter 17

Everything turns toward everything else

Theme: reciprocity, orbit, mirror, exchange.

“one ordered Universe” — Marcus Aurelius

Chapter 18

Home is not a place but a present tense

Theme: the instant becomes dwelling.

“Latitude of Home” — Emily Dickinson

Chapter 19

The whole world says the same thing

Theme: everything echoes the beloved after transformation.

“one Universe out of all” — Marcus Aurelius

Chapter 20

Coda: always / everything

Theme: calm culmination, not melodrama.

“all manner of thing shall be well” / “the other stars” — Julian of Norwich & Dante
